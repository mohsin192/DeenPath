About Qibla Direction

Learn about the importance of Qibla in Islamic prayers

What is Qibla?

Qibla is the direction that Muslims face when performing their prayers (Salah). It is fixed as the direction of the Kaaba in the Great Mosque in Mecca, Saudi Arabia.

The Kaaba is the cube-shaped building at the center of Islam's most important mosque, Al-Masjid Al-Haram, and is the most sacred site in Islam.

Importance of Qibla

  • Unity of Muslims worldwide facing the same direction during prayer
  • Symbol of the oneness of Allah and the unity of the Muslim community
  • Connection to the Prophet Ibrahim (Abraham) and the Kaaba he built
  • Physical manifestation of the spiritual connection between Muslims and Mecca

Finding Qibla Direction

There are several methods to find the Qibla direction:

  • Using a Qibla compass or mobile application
  • Observing the position of the sun (especially on specific dates)
  • Using online Qibla locator tools like this one
  • Observing the direction of mosques in your area

Qibla Calculation

The Qibla direction is calculated using spherical trigonometry based on the great circle path between your location and the Kaaba (21.4225° N, 39.8262° E).

The mathematical formula takes into account the latitude and longitude of both points to determine the precise direction, which varies depending on your geographical location.
